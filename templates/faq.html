ICVerify Help Topics

Guides

  1. Quick-Start Guide


ICVerify is an application to verify matching ICD and CPT codes in patient records. Use ICVerify to search for records in the RIS database and check whether the ICD and CPT codes contained within are errors. To get started:

Step 1. Select the date range

Click on the calendar icons to select the date range for which your desired records exist.

Step 2. Make the search

Click 'Search' to issue a search the RIS for your records. Optionally, click on 'More options' to pick what results show up. "Passed" results are records that do not have errors while "Failed" results do.

Step 3. Understand the results

When the results come back, notice that each result block represents a single record. You can view a record's date, time, accession number, and status. If a record is "failed", you will see a "Not Correct" header in the record block. Here, the ICD and CPT pairs found to be incorrect will be listed.

TIP: If you would like to sort the results, click on the column headers above the results.

Step 4. Make the fix

Switch to the RIS application and correct the ICD/CPT codes. When you make another search on ICVerify, you should notice now that the record does not appear as "failed".

Frequently Asked Questions

  1. How do I expand the view of individual accession numbers?
  2. How do I know if a test result has passed or failed?
  3. How long does it take for the system to update its records?
  4. Can I view passed records?
Can I minimize the view of an individual search record?

Yes, simply click on the row and the row will collapse.

How do I know if a test result has passed or failed?

When viewing the results listings, each accession number will display its status located on the right hand side of the listing, which will display either a "pass" result or a "fail" result

How long does it take for the system to update its records?

Instantaneously. Clicking 'search' will search all records in the RIS at the time of the search.

Can I view passed records?

Yes, click on 'More options' and select the 'passed' checkbox and press 'Search'.
